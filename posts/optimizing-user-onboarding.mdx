<h2>Project Overview</h2>

<div className="col-md-4 pe-md-4">
  <p className="lesson-header">The Product</p>
  <p>
    Funding Societies provides financing for small and medium-sized enterprises.
    Customers can apply directly online on our website. They value speed. To
    improve the time it takes for loans to get approved, we automate basic
    checks during onboarding and screening through this Instant Approval
    feature.
  </p>
</div>

<div className="col-md-4 px-md-4">
  <p className="lesson-header">The Problem</p>
  <p>
    30–40% of web application submissions contain errors in critical
    identification fields. This delays processing time for customers and even
    results in rejections. To fully leverage our{" "}
    <a href="https://samantha.design/work/loan-origination-system">
      automated underwriting tool
    </a>
    , we need to ensure that users’ data is clean and standardised. We want to make
    an “instant” promise to customers, but we are playing catch up with customers'
    expectations.
  </p>
</div>

<div className="col-md-4 ps-md-4">
  <p className="lesson-header">The Solution</p>
  <p>
    We use a third-party identity document verification tool,
    <a href="https://onfido.com/"> Onfido</a> to quickly verify a user’s identity
    with just their ID and face. Our hypothesis is one of the reasons why users submit
    incorrect information is that after they upload their ID, their ID information
    is parsed into the application form fields. Users assume that the parsing is
    accurate and don’t correct it if there are typos.
  </p>
</div>

</div>

<div className="container-fluid margin-top-36">
  <h2>Understand & Analyze</h2>
  <div className="row d-flex justify-content-between">
    <div className="col-md-4 padding-right-24">
      <p className="lesson-header">Customer Pain Points</p>
      <ol>
        <li>
          After users upload their ID and it appears on the web application that
          some fields are auto-populated, users don’t expect that they will have
          to edit them.
        </li>
        <li>
          The users finds it difficult to notice misspellings inside the form
          fields since the application process is very long and the user gets
          fatigued.
        </li>
      </ol>
    </div>
    <div className="col-md-8">
      <LoopingVideo src="/images/work/loan-origination-system/contextual-inquiry.mp4" />
      <figcaption>Previous onboarding flow</figcaption>
    </div>
  </div>

<div className="container-fluid margin-top-36">
  <div className="row d-flex">
    <p className="lesson-header">Solutions solving a similar problem</p>
    <div className="col-md-4 pe-md-4 pb-5">
      <p>
        <b>Wise</b>
        <br />
        Tooltip emphasises the consequences for inaccurate information. However,
        since this tooltip is there all the time, which might annoy users. Our
        customers run their own businesses and are professionals so they might
        feel like they are being talked "down" to.
      </p>
      <br />
      <LoopingVideo src="/images/work/optimizing-onboarding/wise.mp4" />
    </div>
    <div className="col-md-4 px-md-4">
      <p>
        <b>A11Y Style Guide</b>
        <br />
        When tooltip only appears after a short time has passed, it gives enough
        time to the user to realize where the tooltip is coming from. This
        solution is best suited when users don’t know how to fill the form
        field. In our case, users definitely know what ID information is.
      </p>
      <br />
      <LoopingVideo src="/images/work/optimizing-onboarding/a11y.mp4" />
    </div>
    <div className="col-md-4 ps-md-3 pb-5">
      <p>
        <b>Medium</b>
        <br />
        This tooltip has an OK call-to-action to dismiss the modal.
      </p>
